Introductory Probability Theory

Many quantities in real life are difficult to determine exactly - mostly this is due to noise, but sometimes, the quantities themselves are inherently random. In either case, studying probability allows us to model and predict the behaviour of systems with these so-called stochastic variables.

These notes will cover some basic concepts of probability theory.

Topics:

  • The Axioms of Probability Theory
  • Random Variables: Probability Density Functions, Mean, and Variance
  • Bayesian Infrencing

Note: These notes assume a basic understanding of Set Theory.
